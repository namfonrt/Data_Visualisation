Is Australia the land of opportunity for migrants?

By Nutthika Rattanakornkul, MSc Data Science, University of Southampton

Nowadays, globalisation is making the world a better place. Modern transportations help people moving everywhere easier, cheaper and faster. Migration policies of many countries can contribute to economic growth in both home and host countries in terms of filling critical labour shortage and providing tax. Support from governments in host countries makes international migration becomes reality.


According to the recent data presented by the United Nations in 2017 shows the figures of people who move from their original country to another country. This can be called as international migration. Australia is considered as one of the world’s major immigration nations. It was in the 9th rank of International countries migrants destination in 2017.The questions raising in your mind may be “Where are most of the Australian migrants from?, What are they doing in Australia?

Source: United Nation; International migrant stock: The 2017 revision; Total international migrant stock

Source: Australian Bureau of Statistics; Migration, Australia, 2015-16; Estimated Resident Population by Country of Birth - 30 June 1992 to 2016


We used the status of migrants data and the Australian resident population data from the Australian Bureau of Statistics, released in June 2016 which combines data from 1875 to 2016. Over the past ten years, the rate of Australian who born overseas has increased significantly while the percentage of Australian has steadily changed.

As of 2016, more than a quarter of the estimated resident population(ERP), or 6.8 million people, were born overseas.

Where do most of the Australian migrants from?

Source: Australian Bureau of Statistics; Migration, Australia, 2015-16; Estimated Resident Population by Country of Birth - 30 June 1992 to 2016


The number of international migrants in Australia used in this report shows that Australian migrants are from around the world, starting from 1992 until 2016.By using the slider in the map chart, the year setting can be changed. It can be seen that Australia has diverse cultural backgrounds and heritages. At the beginning of the time (1992), the majority of migrants were from Southern and Eastern Europe. Interestingly, Italy and Greece were in the 2nd and the 3rd rank respectively.

In 1996 there were noticeable changes, with the largest proportion of overseas-born residents were from England at almost a million people. It was near-triple over the number of people from New Zealand. Moreover, that was the first year of migrants from Malaysian moving to Australia and it were in the 10th rank.

From 2005 to 2008, there were more people from North-East Asia and southern and central Asia, especially China and India. The number increased at a faster pace.

Over the two decades, the proportion of Australian born in England deceased from 5.1% in 1997 to 4.1% in 2016. Conversely, the proportion of migrants born in Asia increased dramatically.

Aussies more Asian than European

However,from top 15 countries of origin at June 2016, about one in every four migrants in Australia still come from England.This was following by people born in neighbouring New Zealand (2.5%), China (2.1%) , India (1.9%) and Phillipines (1.0)%.


How about the major group and visa, what brings them to Australia?

Source: Australian Bureau of Statistics; Migration, Australia, 2015-16; Net overseas migration, Arrivals and Departures, State/territory, Major groupings and visa - Calendar years, 2004 to 2015


The Net overseas migration data from 2004 to June 2016 shows that, surprisingly, the biggest contribution to a number of migration was from people on temporary visas, not permanent migration, particularly overseas students , visitor and temporary skilled migrants. On the other hand, in just 10 years the number of Australian citizens declined approximately two hundred thousand people.

“Australians must have priority for Australian jobs – so we’re abolishing the [class] 457 visas, the visas that bring temporary foreign workers into our country,”

In the previous decade, the skill visa 457 was introduced by the government, in order to bring the skilled workers to Australia to fill some specific jobs that were facing labour shortage problem. With regards to the number of migrants continue increasing, in 2017 the Australian government denounced the policy to prioritise Australian employment and with new Temporary Skill Shortage that allows migration for two or four years.


Take home Messages
